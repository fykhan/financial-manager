# GradPlan user guide

GradPlan helps you plan and track your money as a new graduate — what comes in, what goes
out, what you owe, and what you're saving toward. This guide walks through everything the app
does. You can jump straight to a section from the sidebar at any time.

---

## The big idea

GradPlan keeps two kinds of information side by side:

- **Your plan** — recurring things you expect: income, recurring expenses, subscriptions,
  loan installments, and savings goals. These describe what *should* happen each month.
- **What actually happened** — individual transactions logged against your accounts.

The **Dashboard** blends both into a single picture: your monthly cash flow, savings rate,
what's due soon, and how last month actually went.

Everything is stored in your account on the server, so it's the same on every device you log
in from. There is no offline mode — you need to be signed in.

---

## Getting started

When your account is empty the Dashboard shows a **Get started** checklist. Work through it in
order:

1. **Add an account** — a bank account, wallet, or credit card. Balances are tracked here.
2. **Add your income** — salary, freelance, or anything recurring.
3. **Add a recurring expense** — rent, groceries, bills.
4. **Set a savings goal** — an emergency fund, a trip, anything.
5. **Log your first transaction** — a real payment or deposit.

In a hurry? Click **Load sample data** to fill the app with a realistic example you can poke
around in, then erase it later from **⚙ Data**.

## Logging transactions

A transaction is a single movement of money. Log one from anywhere:

- The **+ Log** button in the top bar (always visible).
- The **+** button in the mobile bottom bar.
- Keyboard shortcut **n**.

The form is built for speed:

- **Amount first**, so you can start typing straight away.
- **Category chips** for your most-used categories this quarter — tap to fill.
- **Today / Yesterday** buttons for the date.
- **Description autocomplete** — start typing and pick a past description to prefill its
  category, account, and amount.
- **Save & add another** keeps the date and account so you can log a batch quickly.

### Transaction types

- **Expense** — money leaving an account.
- **Income** — money arriving.
- **Transfer** — moving money between two of your own accounts.
- **Savings** — contributing to or withdrawing from a savings goal.
- **Debt** — lending, borrowing, or repaying money you track under Debts.

### Custom categories

Pick **Other…** in the category dropdown to type your own category name. It's remembered and
will show up as a chip and a filter afterwards.

## Accounts & Transactions

**Accounts** shows a card per account with its current balance, your total cash, credit owed,
overall balance, and net worth over time. Click a card (or its **⧩ Filter** button) to show
only that account's transactions.

**Transactions** is your full ledger. Use the controls at the top to find anything:

- **Search** by description, category, or notes.
- **Category chips** to filter to one category.
- **Account dropdown** to filter to one account.

Press **/** anywhere to jump here and focus the search box.

---

## Planning tools

- **Income** — every income source, normalized to a monthly figure. Link an account to
  **auto-deposit** it on its next date.
- **Expenses** — recurring costs by category, plus **Budgets** (a monthly spending limit per
  category, tracked against what you actually spent).
- **Installments** — loans and payment plans, with an amortization preview and payoff timeline.
- **Subscriptions** — anything that renews on a cycle.
- **Savings** — goals with a target and optional deadline; log contributions and withdrawals
  and watch progress fill in.
- **Debts** — money you owe or are owed, per person.

Amounts are always stored in their **original frequency** (weekly, monthly, yearly…) and
converted to a monthly figure only when shown, so nothing is ever silently rounded.

### Auto-pay and "Due soon"

If you link an account to an income, expense, subscription, or installment, GradPlan posts it
automatically when its date arrives. Anything **not** linked shows up under **Due soon** on the
Dashboard with a **Mark paid** button — click it to log the payment and roll its next date
forward in one step.

## The Dashboard

- **Tiles** — monthly income, monthly expenses (with a *"vs last month"* spend comparison),
  net cash flow, and savings rate.
- **Last month in review** — money in and out, top spending categories, and your biggest
  single expense. Click **Full statement →** to open it in detail.
- **Where your money goes** — a category breakdown you can switch between planned and actual.
- **Due soon**, **budgets**, and **recent activity** at a glance.

---

## Statement

The **Statement** view is a proper period report — pick **This month**, **Last month**, a
quarter, a year, or a custom date range. When you're viewing a single month, use the **‹ ›**
buttons to step month by month. Export it as **CSV** or **print / save as PDF**.

---

## Your data

Open **⚙ Data** in the sidebar to:

- **Export** a JSON backup or a CSV spreadsheet.
- **Import** a JSON backup onto another device.
- **Print** the current view as a PDF.
- **Load sample data** or **Erase everything**.

> Changing the **display currency** only changes the symbol shown — it does **not** convert
> your amounts. Values are stored and exported exactly as you entered them.

Deleted something by accident? Every delete shows an **Undo** button for a few seconds.

---

## Appearance & shortcuts

- **◐ Theme** toggles light and dark.
- **▓ Effects** switches between the full neon look and a calmer, subtler one (per device).
- **Install** GradPlan to your home screen or desktop from your browser's install prompt — it
  runs in its own window like an app.

### Keyboard shortcuts

- **n** — log a new transaction
- **/** — jump to Transactions and focus search
- **g** then **d** / **t** / **a** / **s** — go to Dashboard / Transactions / Accounts / Statement

---

That's everything. Your data is saved the moment you enter it — just start logging.
